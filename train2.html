TGV France

The TGV (Train à Grande Vitesse) is France's flagship high-speed train, known worldwide for its speed, efficiency, and innovation in rail transport. Launched by the French national railway company SNCF in 1981, the TGV was designed to connect major cities across France and, eventually, to neighboring countries. Capable of reaching speeds of up to 320 km/h (199 mph), it has transformed travel by significantly reducing journey times. With its streamlined design, the TGV is not only fast but also highly energy-efficient, relying primarily on electric power. Over the years, the TGV network has expanded, linking France to Belgium, Switzerland, Italy, Germany, and Spain. Inside, the TGV offers comfortable seating, quiet zones, family areas, and a café-bar, creating a pleasant environment for both business and leisure travelers.

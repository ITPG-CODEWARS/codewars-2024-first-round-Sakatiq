Thalys PBKA

The Thalys PBKA is a high-speed train that connects major cities in France, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Germany—hence its name, which stands for Paris, Brussels, Köln (Cologne), and Amsterdam. Developed by Alstom based on the TGV technology, the Thalys PBKA trains were introduced in 1996 and are known for their impressive speeds, reaching up to 300 km/h (186 mph), making cross-border travel in Western Europe quick and efficient.

Designed for international routes, the PBKA trains are compatible with multiple electrical systems used across different countries, enabling seamless travel across national borders. The interior is thoughtfully designed for passenger comfort, with spacious seating in both First and Second Class, quiet zones, power outlets, and free Wi-Fi. The train’s distinctive red exterior and streamlined, aerodynamic shape make it instantly recognizable. The Thalys PBKA has not only enhanced connectivity across Europe but also set a standard for efficient, eco-friendly, and convenient international rail travel.

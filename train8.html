AVE Class 100

The AVE Class 100, also known as the "Serie 100," is a high-speed train developed by Alstom for Spain's Renfe railway operator. Introduced in 1992, it was Spain's first high-speed train, initially designed for the Madrid-Seville route, significantly reducing travel time and revolutionizing rail travel in Spain.

With a top operational speed of 300 km/h (186 mph), the Class 100 is known for its reliability, comfort, and aerodynamic design inspired by France’s TGV technology.

Inside, the AVE Class 100 offers two classes: Turista (Standard) and Preferente (First Class), with spacious seating, ample legroom, and modern amenities. The train features soundproofing, air conditioning, and a dedicated café area, ensuring a comfortable experience for long journeys.

Though newer high-speed trains have joined the AVE fleet, the Class 100 remains an important part of Spain's rail history, laying the groundwork for the extensive high-speed rail network we know today.
